90's Hip Hop vs. 20's Hip Hop

Introduction

Hip hop has undoubtedly evolved over the years, reflecting the changing times and cultural shifts. In this blog, we will explore the key differences and similarities between 90's hip hop and the hip hop of the 20's.

The Sound

One of the most noticeable differences between 90's hip hop and 20's hip hop is the sound. In the 90's, hip hop was characterized by heavy beats, catchy hooks, and distinct samples from funk, soul, and R&B music. Artists like Tupac Shakur, The Notorious B.I.G., Busta Rhymes, and Wu-Tang Clan dominated the scene with their raw and gritty sound.


On the other hand, 20's hip hop has taken a more diverse approach to its sound. It incorporates elements from various genres, such as trap, EDM, and even rock. Artists like Kendrick Lamar, Drake, all the way down to Playboi Cart and Lil Uzi Vert, have experimented with different styles, resulting in a more eclectic and versatile sound in contemporary hip hop.

Lyrics and Themes

Another significant difference between 90's hip hop and 20's hip hop lies in the lyrics and themes explored by the artists. In the 90's, hip hop was often characterized by socially conscious lyrics that addressed issues like racism, police brutality, and social inequality. Artists used their music as a platform for activism and bringing attention to the struggles faced by marginalized communities.


In the 20's, hip hop still addresses social issues, but the lyrical content has expanded to include a wider range of topics. Artists delve into personal experiences, relationships, mental health, and self-expression. The focus has shifted towards individuality, empowerment, and embracing one's uniqueness.

Fashion and Visuals

When it comes to fashion and visuals, both eras of hip hop have made their mark. In the 90's, baggy clothes, oversized jackets, bucket hats, and gold chains were iconic fashion statements. Music videos showcased urban environments and street culture, often portraying a gritty aesthetic that reflected the artists' realities.


In the 20's, hip hop fashion has become more diverse and influenced by high fashion as well as the 90's fashion. Artists experiment with various styles, blending streetwear with designer brands. Now, you may see guys wearing long scarves over their heads, handbags, and satchels (a.k.a purses). However, they generally find ways to still make it look cool, and highly fashionable. Visuals in music videos have also become more extravagant and cinematic, incorporating advanced special tech effects and artistic storytelling.


The Impact

Both 90's hip hop and 20's hip hop have had a profound impact on music, culture, and society. The 90's era laid the foundation for many future artists, establishing hip hop as a mainstream genre, while the 20's continue to push the boundaries of hip hop to unimaginable heights. But beyond fashion, hip hop is now the modern day leaders of society. Whatever trends they set, fans of pop culture follow. It never gets boring watching their influence over the world!
